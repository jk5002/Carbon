The Problem

📋 Companies over-reporting Scope 2

Current methodology uses annual-average grid emission factors. It ignores when electricity is consumed. Half-hourly DNO-matching can produce 8–40% lower Scope 2 figures without using less electricity.

📜 Regulatory pressure is accelerating

SECR mandatory for large UK companies. UK SRS mandatory for listed companies from Jan 2027. FCA expected to extend to large private companies. Supply chain Scope 3 pressure pulling mid-market in.

📊 Data exists — but nobody uses it properly

Smart meters produce half-hourly readings. DCC infrastructure covers 30M+ meters. National Grid ESO publishes live carbon intensity per DNO region. The pipes are built — the product layer is missing.

🔑 The Gap

Most carbon platforms use monthly averages or manual uploads. None of the UK mid-market tools do true half-hourly DNO-region matching from live smart meter feeds. The GHG Protocol is converging on this standard for 2027.

📜 SECR

Mandatory for large UK companies & LLPs. Scope 1 & 2 GHGs in tCO₂e, energy consumption by fuel type, intensity ratios. All fields auto-generated.

Mandatory for listed companies from Jan 2027. Expected to extend to large private companies. CarbonLedger's granularity exceeds proposed requirements.

Honest Risks

⚠ Not first to market

Openvolt already does the core technical function. Need a sharper wedge — portfolio analytics, BMS depth, or a specific vertical like commercial real estate.

⚠ Data coverage gaps

~36% of non-domestic properties lack smart meters. A client with 30 sites might have clean data for only 18. BMS integration is inconsistent.

⚠ Scope 2 alone isn't enough

Every prospect will ask "what about Scope 1 and 3?" Either expand scope or position clearly as a data layer that feeds existing platforms.

⚠ Long sales cycles

SECR-obligated companies buy through procurement/finance teams with ESG consultants involved. Expect 3–6 month cycles. High churn risk if key contact leaves.

⚠ Audit-readiness is not trivial

Multiplying kWh by carbon intensity is easy. Making the output genuinely audit-ready with data lineage, gap-fill methodology, and ISAE 3000 support requires significant investment.

⚠ "We already use X"

A meaningful percentage of SECR-obligated companies already have a carbon platform. Getting them to switch or pay for an additional layer is a real barrier.

Go-To-Market Strategy

🎯 Primary Channel: B2B Partnerships

Sell into energy brokers, sustainability consultancies, or property management platforms rather than direct to end-clients. Positions granular data as an enhancement to an existing workflow — not a standalone tool competing with full-suite platforms like IBM Envizi or Watershed.

⚡ The Real Differentiator

Not annual SECR compliance — it's real-time operational decision support. Shift flexible loads to low-carbon hours. Benchmark across building estate. Identify energy waste with precision. Demonstrate actual reductions — not accounting changes.

🔌 Wedge: GHG Protocol 2027 Readiness

The standard is converging on hourly matching. Companies that adopt half-hourly DNO-matched reporting now get a 2-year compliance head start. That's a genuine selling point for forward-thinking sustainability teams.

📋 Supply Chain Pull

Even mid-sized companies are being pulled into enterprise Scope 3 reporting. Demonstrable energy data management becomes a procurement differentiator — not just compliance, but competitive advantage in supply chains.

Speed matters. This niche will attract well-capitalised competitors fast. A defensible data moat is essential.
