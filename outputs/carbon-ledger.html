How It Works

From meter to report in four steps

No manual data entry. No spreadsheets. No consultants required for Scope 2.

1

Connect Your Meters

Plug into your smart meters via Procode's IDA or similar — DCC-accredited, covering SMETS1 & SMETS2 across 30M+ UK meters. Or connect BMS/DDC feeds for larger buildings.

2

Match Carbon Intensity

Every half-hourly kWh reading is automatically matched to the regional carbon intensity for that DNO area, pulled live from the National Grid ESO API.

3

Detect Anomalies

Loop Optimise or similar automatically flags phantom loads, unusual consumption patterns, and data gaps — giving you energy intelligence, not just carbon numbers.

4

Generate SECR Reports

One click produces a fully formatted, audit-ready SECR report with data lineage, gap-fill methodology, intensity ratios, and year-on-year comparatives.

Compliance

Built for UK regulatory requirements

CarbonCode produces reports aligned with every major UK and international carbon reporting standard.

📜

SECR (Streamlined Energy & Carbon Reporting)

Mandatory for large UK companies & LLPs. Covers global Scope 1 & 2, energy consumption by fuel type, intensity ratios, and methodology. All produced automatically.

🏛️

UK SRS (Sustainability Reporting Standards)

Mandatory for listed companies from January 2027. Expected to extend to large private companies. CarbonCode's data granularity exceeds the proposed requirements.

🌐

GHG Protocol — Scope 2 Guidance

Formal review underway with new hourly matching & deliverability requirements expected in final 2027 standard. CarbonCode is already compliant with the proposed update.

ISAE 3000 Assurance-Ready

Data lineage from source reading to final tCO₂e figure. Complete audit trail with gap-fill reason codes, emission factor versions, and invoice reconciliation — built for limited assurance.

🏢

ESOS (Energy Savings Opportunity Scheme)

Mandatory energy assessments for large organisations. CarbonCode's half-hourly data and anomaly detection feeds directly into ESOS compliance workflows.

FAQ

Common questions

❓

What if some of my sites don't have smart meters?

Approximately 64% of UK non-domestic properties have smart meters. For sites without them, CarbonCode accepts manual uploads, BMS/DDC feeds, or half-hourly settlement data from your energy supplier. Our gap-fill methodology is fully documented for audit purposes.

❓

Does this cover Scope 1 and Scope 3 as well?

CarbonCode is purpose-built for Scope 2, where the half-hourly DNO-matching methodology adds the most value. We provide data exports and APIs that feed into broader ESG platforms covering Scope 1 and Scope 3. Full Scope 1 & 3 support is on our roadmap.

❓

How do you handle data gaps from meter outages?

Gaps under 2 hours use linear interpolation. Longer gaps use DNO-weekday-historical averages. Every filled interval is flagged with a reason code in the audit trail. Our invoice reconciliation step ensures the total kWh aligns with your supplier bills within 1% tolerance.

❓

Is the National Grid ESO API reliable?

The API is free, well-documented, and returns live JSON data with a 98%+ uptime record. CarbonCode caches carbon intensity data locally as a fallback, and historical data is independently verifiable via the National Grid's published datasets.
